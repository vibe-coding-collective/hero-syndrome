# Hero Syndrome — Concept

## Premise

A mobile web app that generates a continuous, adaptive soundtrack to your ongoing life, using your phone's sensors as emotional telemetry. You open it, it plays. You walk outside, the strings swell. A storm rolls in, the key modulates. You drop a 🔥 onto the waveform and the tempo kicks up. When you close the session, the whole thing saves as an **episode** of your life — titled, timestamped, shareable.

## The experience

A session is a single continuous stretch of music that adapts every ~30–60 seconds as your state changes.

1. Open the app. It asks for location and motion permissions.
2. Tap **Start scene**. Audio begins within a few seconds.
3. Music plays through speakers or headphones. A waveform visualization responds to the audio.
4. You go about your life. Movement, weather, location, and time of day silently shape the composition.
5. You can drag mood stickers onto the waveform — 🔥 🌧️ 👁️ 💀 🌀 — to inject intent. Stickers decay over time.
6. When you end the scene, the session is saved as an **Episode**: a title, a date, a sparse visual timeline of signals and stickers, and the stitched soundtrack.
7. Episodes live at shareable URLs. Send to a friend, revisit later.

## The signals

The state vector is the narrative spine. Each signal is chosen because it already carries emotional semantics:

- **Time of day** — dawn, golden hour, witching hour. Phase-tagged, not hour-tagged.
- **Weather** — temperature, condition, sunrise/sunset proximity.
- **Location semantics** — not coordinates. "Walking in a park." "In a vehicle on an urban street." "Still, near water."
- **Movement intensity** — accelerometer-derived. Classified as *still / walking / running / vehicle* plus a continuous intensity value.
- **Mood stickers** — your editorial voice in the score.

An LLM reads the vector and writes a short, evocative music prompt. ElevenLabs Music turns it into a clip. A two-clip buffer with crossfade makes the stream continuous; every clip boundary is a chance for the music to shift.

## Mood stickers

Stickers are the user's agency layer. They don't directly map to parameters — they enter the LLM as intent tokens alongside the state vector. The LLM decides what *more intense* or *more dramatic about rain* actually sounds like.

Each sticker has a lifetime of ~5 minutes. It fades visually and narratively. This means the user's influence always dissolves back into data — the score is mostly the world, occasionally you.

The palette is small and curated. A dozen or two emojis, chosen for emotional range. Not hundreds.

## The episode

Each session becomes an episode.

- Title generated by LLM from the signal timeline (e.g. *"Walking home, rain starting, three stickers deep"*).
- Visual timeline: speed, weather, sticker events.
- Audio: the stitched, crossfaded soundtrack.
- Shareable URL with an opaque ID.

No accounts. No login. Episodes live by URL.

## Design principles

1. **Earnest, not cute.** The premise is absurd on its face; the execution should be sincere.
2. **Data is material, not truth.** Signals exist to drive music, not to be accurate. Don't render dashboards.
3. **Latency is part of the art.** Music responds in beats, not frames. Don't over-optimize.
4. **No accounts, no onboarding, no friction.** Open, permit, play.
5. **Shareable output.** The episode is the thing people send to friends.

## What it is not

- Not a music generator (you're not prompting songs).
- Not a DAW (you're not composing).
- Not a fitness or wellness app.
- Not an always-on ambient layer (the platform won't allow it, and the ritual is better).
